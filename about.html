Bio

Jennifer Olson was born in southern California and raised in Altadena. She has three siblings, one of which is an older sister. She enjoyed singing and dancing with her family as she grew up, especially for a group called the campfire girls. They would host craft shows and concerts, which were partially funded by a local pizza place. Her childhood centered on spending time with her family and friends, especially when it came to singing activities and playing outside in the family pool.

Throughout her schooling, Jennifer attended various camps, including one in Ore, Minnesota. She applied the incentive of neer giving up when doing various activities, which resulted in her learning new things from these experiences. Jennifer attended private schools in Pasadena, graduating from Imperial Schools with a class of 36 students. While a student there, she had the privilege of singing in concerts with the Little Ambassadors of Shanghai, who had come over from China, sponsored by the Ambassador Foundation. They were able to perform in Pasadena, San Francisco, and Washington, D.C. in the Kennedy Center.

Jennifer worked her way through high school and college. When she was seventeen and eighteen years old, she taught gymnastics with her older sister. For a while, she was also the secretary of the Architecture and Engineering section of the church organization she and her family attended. She worked in the kitchen of the Ambassador College campus, during which time one of her pastimes was playing volleyball. This ended, however, when she suffered a knee injury in her freshman year of college. One of her teachers gave her housing for about a week so she could recover from this injury, and began working at the dining room console, calling and paging people in the area. In her senior year, she had to resume working in the kitchen due to students getting loans.

Eventually, Jennifer was hired at a home loans company in the area, where she worked as a floater. Basically, this meant assisting other employees with jobs as they needed help. After she graduated from college, this job became full-time, and she was trained more fully on how to endorse and deposit checks. After her department was consolidated into the real estate management department of the home loans company, she became certified as a team leader in the property claims department.

In 2001, she and her husband decided to move to Wisconsin, so she quit her job at the home loans company. Though she never again held a full-time job, she focused on finding ways to save money for her family, including entering surveys and sweepstakes.
